What is Cryptocurrency? A cryptocurrency is one medium of exchange, like traditional currencies such as USD. It is still designed to exchange digital information through a process made possible by certain principles of cryptography. It is a digital currency that is classified as a subset of alternative currencies and virtual currencies. How do Cryptocurrencies Work? To create a cryptocurrency, you need to solve a complex mathematical problem called a blockchain. This is done by using a network of computers that all share the same set of instructions. Once the problem is solved, the miner who found the solution is rewarded with cryptocurrency. Cryptocurrencies are unique in that they can be transferred between users without involving any third party. This makes them very secure and prevents anyone from being able to steal or misuse them. Cryptocurrencies are stored in digital wallets, which can be accessed on computers or mobile devices. Why learn Crypto?

Amazon Interview Experience | Set 427 (SDE-2) Josh Technology Group Recruitment Process | Pool Drive Truminds Interview Experience for SDE (On-Campus) View Discussion Improve Article Save Article Like Article Last Updated : 29 Jul, 2022 Round 1: It was an online test of two hours duration on HackerEarth platform. Consisted of 18 MCQs from CS subjects – Operating Systems, DSA, Aptitude, DBMS. The questions were of easy level. 1 coding question was asked which was of medium level. A variation to this question: https://www.geeksforgeeks.org/number-subsequences-string-divisible-n/ Round 2: It was a technical interview of about 45 minutes. Questions were from Operating Systems, Data Structures and Algorithms, DBMS, Web Development, Object Oriented Programming, C++, Python, and coding. Introduce Yourself Easy to medium-level questions were asked like – Inheritance concepts ( I was asked to code Base class and Derived class program by sharing my screen), Paging in OS, Serializability in DBMS, Real-time operating system, and some other questions Swap two numbers without using a third variable, write syntax of data structure which can store an integer and a character data types, About virtual functions, basic SQL queries What are React.js and Angular.js Do you have any questions for me? Round 3: Leadership Discussion – It was also a technical cum personality round where basic CS questions were asked and presentation skills were tested. The duration was around 20 minutes. Introduce Yourself Friend functions in c++, templates in c++ Write a program in C/C++ to print hello world such that main() function should be empty: https://www.geeksforgeeks.org/print-hello-world-empty-blank-main-c/ AVL trees, commLinuxin linux, what is ping command, IP addressing in computer networks, MAC add the easing, difference between TCP and UDP, Interposes Communication Virtual Memory, Segmentation in OS Do you have any questions for me? Overall it was a good experience with healthy discussion on core subjects, programming and new technologies. FINAL VERDICT – SELECTED AND ACCEPTED THE OFFER Around 20 students from our campus applied for Truminds, from which around 12 were selected for Round 2. After Round 2, 6 students made it to Round 3. Finally, 5 students got the offer.

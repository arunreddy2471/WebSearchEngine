RPA stands for ‚ÄúRobotic Process Automation.‚Äù It is a type of artificial intelligence that uses bots to do the work of humans. These bots can repeatedly perform repetitive, simple tasks, and RPA is a powerful tool because it can automate all mundane business tasks. The main objective of the RPA tutorial for a beginner is to decrease costs and improve efficiency by doing tasks that people traditionally do. Why learn Robotic Process Automation? Here are some prominent reasons for learning RPA: Multiple processes can be automated at once. Cost-cutting technology that helps you to enhance resource optimization. Supports and allows regular compliance process, with error-free auditing. Easy to model, scale, and deploy the automation process. It makes it easy to track defects. Continuous builds & release management. You do not need much training as it works without human intervention. RPA will not work in small industries. Makes accurate and timely reports by automating the process of extracting data. Reduces the gap between systems by preventing custom implementations. Delivers quality products by performing testing and automating customer use case scenarios. Transfer data between disparate and legacy systems Applications of RPA Here are some important applications of RPA: Accounting: For general accounting, operational accounting, transaction, and reporting. Customer Services: By support center duties, can assist businesses to deliver improved customer service, including e-signature verification, scanned records uploading, and data verification. Healthcare: It can be used by medical organizations for handling patient records, account management, reporting, and data analytics. Human Resources: It helps you automate HR functions, including onboarding and offboarding, staff updates, and submission procedures of schedules. Financial Services: Financial services companies use RPA for foreign exchange payments, Automation of account opening and closing, and audit request management.
